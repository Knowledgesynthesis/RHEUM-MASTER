# **3. Curriculum Map & Knowledge Graph**

## **Module 1: Rheumatoid Arthritis**
- Morning stiffness, symmetric polyarthritis  
- Small joints first  
- RF & anti-CCP interpretation  
- X-ray findings (erosions, joint narrowing)  
- Extra-articular manifestations  
- DMARD logic (educational): methotrexate → biologics conceptually  

## **Module 2: Systemic Lupus Erythematosus (SLE)**
- Constitutional + mucocutaneous + serositis + renal clues  
- ANA subtypes (anti-dsDNA, anti-Smith)  
- Complement levels (C3/C4)  
- Lupus nephritis patterns  
- Photosensitivity, malar rash, oral ulcers  
- Disease activity trends  

## **Module 3: Vasculitides**
### **Giant Cell Arteritis (GCA)**
- Age > 50  
- Temporal headache, jaw claudication  
- Elevated ESR/CRP  
- Vision loss emergency  

### **Takayasu Arteritis**
- Age < 40  
- Limb claudication  
- Decreased pulses, BP differences  
- Large-vessel imaging (conceptual)  

### **Polyarteritis Nodosa (PAN)**
- Medium-vessel involvement  
- Renal, GI, neuro ischemia  
- HBV association overview  

### **ANCA-Associated Vasculitis**
- GPA vs MPA (c-ANCA/PR3 vs p-ANCA/MPO conceptual)  
- Upper airway involvement  
- Pulmonary-renal syndrome  
- Hematuria, proteinuria clues  

## **Module 4: Crystal Arthropathies**
### **Gout**
- Podagra, needle-shaped negatively birefringent crystals  
- Triggers (diet, dehydration)  
- Chronic tophi  
- Uric acid pathophysiology  

### **Pseudogout**
- Knee involvement  
- Rhomboid, positively birefringent crystals  
- Calcium pyrophosphate deposition  

## **Module 5: Ankylosing Spondylitis (AS)**
- Inflammatory back pain  
- Morning stiffness improving with exercise  
- Sacroiliitis  
- HLA-B27 association  
- Bamboo spine imaging (conceptual)  

Knowledge Graph:
Symptoms → Joint pattern → Systemic signs → Labs → Imaging → Diagnosis → Conceptual Treatment.

# **6. Rheumatologic Reasoning Framework**

Universal logic:
1. Identify **pattern of joint involvement** (mono vs oligo vs poly; symmetric vs asymmetric).  
2. Determine **inflammatory vs mechanical**.  
3. Screen for **systemic features** (rash, fever, neuropathy, renal dysfunction).  
4. Order targeted **serologies** (RF, anti-CCP, ANA, ANCA, complements).  
5. Evaluate **organ involvement** (renal, pulmonary, neuro, skin).  
6. Apply high-yield diagnostic criteria conceptually.  
7. Choose conceptual treatment strategy tier (NSAIDs → DMARDs → biologic pathways).  
8. Monitor progression & potential complications.

**Pitfalls to highlight:**
- Confusing lupus arthritis with RA  
- Missing GCA vision risk  
- Misinterpreting ANCA patterns  
- Overlooking medium-vessel clues in PAN  
- Confusing pseudogout with septic arthritis  
- Mistaking mechanical back pain for inflammatory AS  
- Overdiagnosing lupus based on ANA alone
